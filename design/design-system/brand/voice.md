# Voice, tone & the Five Laws

This file lives inside the design system so writers and designers can grab it without diving into the docx. Source: `Delngato_Brand_Guidelines_v1.1.docx`.

## The voice in one paragraph

دلنجاتُو بتكلم الناس زي صاحب شاطر — مش شركة، مش روبوت. كلام واضح، محترم، وعارف إيه اللي محتاجه. مش بنبالغ ومش بنتكلم من فوق.

> Delngato talks to people like a smart friend. Not a company, not a robot. Clear, respectful, knows what's needed. We don't exaggerate and we don't talk down.

## Five voice principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **واضح — Clear** | قول اللي عايز تقوله بدون دوران. |
| **واثق — Confident** | مش "ممكن" أو "يمكن" — قول بصراحة. |
| **بشري — Human** | كلام ناس حقيقية، مش template. |
| **محلي — Local** | بنعرف الدلنجات ومش بنتكلم زي اللي في القاهرة. |
| **مختصر — Brief** | الوقت محترم — أقل كلمات وأكتر معنى. |

## Brand promise

**اطلب من محلات الدلنجات — تلاقي طلبك عندك في دقايق. بدون مكالمة، بدون انتظار، بدون تخمين.**

> Order from El-Delngat shops — you'll find your order at your door in minutes. No call. No wait. No guessing.

## We are / We are not

| We are | We are not |
|---|---|
| Premium | Expensive or exclusive |
| Minimal | Cold or sterile |
| Confident | Arrogant |
| Local | Folkloric or nostalgic |
| Modern | Trendy or gimmicky |
| Fast | Rushed or careless |

## The Five Laws

These are non-negotiable. Every design decision is evaluated against them.

**Law 01 — Arabic First. Always.**
Default experience is Arabic, default direction is RTL, default voice is Egyptian colloquial. English is *available*, never the primary path.
> If it doesn't work in Arabic first, it doesn't work.

**Law 02 — Clarity Over Cleverness.**
Every element — visual, verbal, interactive — must communicate clearly. Clever copy that confuses or animations that distract violate this law.
> If a user has to think twice about what something means, redesign it.

**Law 03 — Premium in Feel. Accessible in Price.**
The look is premium. The product is for everyone in El-Delngat — regardless of income or tech literacy. No paywall, no premium tier in v1.
> If a design choice signals exclusion, remove it.

**Law 04 — One City First. Expansion Second.**
Every decision is made for El-Delngat. Not Cairo, not international, not Phase 2.
> If it's not better for El-Delngat specifically, it's not ready.

**Law 05 — Trust Is Earned by Working.**
Reliability earns trust, not promises. Every delivered order adds one unit of trust. One missed order subtracts ten.
> If it ships broken, it breaks the brand.

## Copy examples (canon)

| Context | Arabic | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding | دلنجاتُو — اطلب من محلات الدلنجات وادّفع من الأبلكيشن. | Names the value in one breath |
| Order confirmed | تم استلام طلبك. بيتجهزلك دلوقتي. | Two short sentences. No exclamation. |
| Out for delivery | طلبك في الطريق — ٧ دقايق تقريباً. | Em-dash, Arabic-Indic numerals, "approximately" |
| Order delivered | تم التوصيل. شكراً لاستخدامك دلنجاتُو. | Polite, not effusive |
| Error | في مشكلة صغيرة. بنحلها. جرب تاني. | Owns the issue. No "sorry" theatre. |
| Empty cart | السلة فاضية. ابدأ تطلب من هنا. | Names the state, gives the next step |
| Push notification | طلبك وصل! افتح الأبلكيشن تأكد. | The one screen where `!` is permitted |

## Language rules (hard)

- Primary: Egyptian colloquial Arabic in UX. MSA only in legal contexts.
- English: same tone, same brevity. Never the default.
- Mixed text: Arabic first; English only for brand names and technical terms.
- Exclamation marks: **maximum one per screen.**
- Emoji: **not in UI copy.** Marketing and social only.
